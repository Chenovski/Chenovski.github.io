[CA] Conformal Maps on SCD are Determined Simply by 2-point-case

CA Originally published 2020-01-28

cf. Greene Ch6 Problem #12 By Riemann Mapping Theorem, any SCD (Simply Connected Domain) is conformally equivalent to the unit disc. So this problem can be considered for automorphisms on the unit disc, by the composition of original conformal map with a conformal map derived from RMT as well as its inverse. Now, on the unit disc, assume we have automorphisms f and g satisfy f(P) = g(P) and f(Q) = g(Q) for distinct P and Q, so we can compose f with g-inverse (or g with f-inverse, etc.), creating a new automorphism on the unit disc, let's say, h. We know h(P) = P and h(Q) = Q. Then, we can easily find a Mobius transform M (in details, w = -1, a = P) mapping P to 0 and 0 to P. Composing this transform and its inverse with h on both sides, the result, say phi, is an automorphism on the unit disc which maps 0 to 0. Then we have a good property waiting for phi, that is, phi should be in the form of wz. On the other hand, M(Q) was mapped to M(Q) itself by phi. Because P and Q are distinct, M(Q) is non-zero, hence w = 1, i.e. phi(z) = z, phi = id. Track all the way back to f = g. We done.
